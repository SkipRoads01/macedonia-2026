North Macedonia
Regional Context
Macedonia sits at the heart of the western Balkans — a small landlocked republic where Ottoman mosques, Byzantine frescoes, Roman ruins, and Slavic Orthodox monasteries occupy the same valleys and hillsides. This itinerary moves south then north, following the geography Rebecca West traced: Skopje and Kosovo, through the central plains to Bitola, west to Ohrid on its ancient lake, and up into the Mavrovo highlands.

SUNDAY
Arrive Skopje
Arrival · Night 1
  • Land at Alexander the Great Airport (SKP)
  • Pick up VW Taigo automatic at Europcar desk
  • Check in, decompress
  • Easy first evening — local dinner, early night (long travel day)
SEP 22
MONDAY
Skopje
Walking / Exploring
  • Old Bazaar (Čaršija) — Ottoman lanes, copper shops, coffee
  • Kale Fortress — views over the Vardar
  • Stone Bridge and riverside
  • Church of St. Spas (intricately carved iconostasis)
  • Skopje 2014 statuary — with appropriate skepticism
  • Long café afternoon with the book
SEP 23
TUESDAY
Kosovo
Day Trip · Return to Skopje
  • Drive north from Skopje into Kosovo (~1.5 hrs to Pristina)
  • Gračanica Monastery (14th c. Serbian Orthodox — extraordinary frescoes; West's Balkans made visible)
  • Pristina — Mother Teresa Square, café, afternoon wander
  • Return to Skopje by evening
  • Bring passport. Kosovo uses EUR. West passed through this landscape in 1937.
SEP 24
WEDNESDAY
Drive to Bitola
Transit · ~2.5 hrs
  • Check out of Skopje
  • Drive south via Veles
  • Optional stop: Stobi (Roman ruins, en route)
  • Check in Bitola by afternoon
  • First evening on Širok Sokak
SEP 24–26
2 NIGHTS
Bitola
2 nights
  • Širok Sokak — café culture, consular row, the boulevard life West describes
  • Heraclea Lyncestis (Roman / Byzantine ruins just south; floor mosaics)
  • Clock Tower and old bazaar quarter
  • Church of Sveti Dimitrija
  • Pelister National Park (day hike, if energy allows)
  • Sit with West's portrait of interwar Bitola over coffee
SEP 26
SATURDAY
Bitola → Ohrid via Treskavec
Transit · via Prilep
  • Check out of Bitola
  • Treskavec Monastery (dramatic hilltop above Prilep — short hike or rough track; worth it)
  • Prilep old town, briefly
  • Continue west to Ohrid ~2 hrs
  • Check in by late afternoon — first look at the lake
SEP 26–29
3 NIGHTS
Ohrid
3 nights · 2 full days
  • Old town — medieval lanes climbing from the lake
  • Church of St. John at Kaneo (the iconic lakeside church; early morning)
  • Church of St. Clement at Plaošnik (remarkable frescoes; ancient foundations dating to the 5th century)
  • Samuel's Fortress — views over the lake
  • Icon Gallery and National Museum
  • Vevčani village (half-day; natural springs, stone houses, deeply traditional)
  • Long walks along the shore; lakeside cafés; boat trip if the mood strikes
SEP 29–OCT 1
2 NIGHTS
Mavrovo / Bigorski
2 nights · Highland
  • Drive north from Ohrid into the Mavrovo highlands (~1.5 hrs)
  • Monastery of St. John Bigorski (18th-c. iconostasis — one of the finest in the Balkans)
  • Galicnik village (above Mavrovo; stone village, extraordinary views, early autumn)
  • Mavrovo Lake and national park walks
  • Autumn colours in the highlands — peak season
  • The most remote and quiet leg of the trip
OCT 1–2
THURSDAY
Return to Skopje
Return · Departure
  • Drive back to Skopje ~2 hrs
  • Final afternoon — anything missed, last café, last walk
  • Return car to Europcar at airport
  • Depart Skopje → Vienna → Boston (Austrian Airlines)
